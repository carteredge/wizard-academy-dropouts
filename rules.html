Conjuring lightning from your fingertips! Summoning skeletons to do your bidding! Peering through time! These and more are things that you absolutely sucked at! Alas, you were good enough to make it into the Wizard Academy, but not good enough to make it out with your degree in Wizarding.

Wizard Academy Dropouts is a table-top role playing game (TTRPG) for two or more players, one of which is the Headmaster (or HM) who runs the game and the rest are the failed wizards (player characters or PCs). Unlike most TTRPGs, the failed wizards are assumed to be both fairly smart and fairly weak — standard nerds. Anything an average person can do physically, they can also do. Anything that takes an above average amount of strength or endurance, they automatically fail to do unless they use magic. Similarly, they have all the wit and genre-savviness the players can muster, and information about their environment should be freely given unless it pertains to magic. Everything else is accomplished through casting spells and knowledge rolls as described below.

Creating Your Character

Final Report Card

Even a failed wizard had to take a few classes to get where they are. Your character's skills in various types of magic are reflected in the final report card they received before being expelled or dropping out from the Wizard Academy. Give yourself two Cs, two Ds, and two Fs in the following classes in their respective disciplines:

Conjuration

Conjuration 101: Creating Something from Nothing — Conjuration can magically produce items, simple and complex.

Elementalism

Elementalism 101: Frost, Fire, Storm, and Stone — Shield yourself with stone, freeze your foes, and blast them with fire and lightning.

Enchantment

Enchantment 101: Enhancement through Enchantment — Magically enhance items or boost someone's physical abilities to supernatural degrees.

Illusion

Illusion 101: Invisibility and Beyond — Create illusions, command shadows, or turn things invisible.

Nautralism

Naturalism 101: Arcane Ecology — Control plants, summon animals, and command the weather.

Necromancy

Necromancy 101: My First Skeletal Minion — Invoke death magic and summon undead minions.

Failure

Decide why your wizard is no longer attending the Wizard Academy:

  • I dropped out. I couldn't focus, and I changed my specialty constantly until my professors couldn't take it anymore. When you make a roll, you can drop the grade for the discipline of that roll by one letter and raise the grade of another discipline by one letter.

  • I was kicked out. My spells are extra dangerous. Your spells deal 1 extra damage.

  • I failed out. I'm so bad at magic, I'm resistant to it. Prevent 1 magical damage each round.

Knack

Choose one of the following traits as your character's knack (a thing your character in fact does not suck at):

  • Bardic – Outside of combat, you can use double the number of words for a spell if the words are phrased as a rhyming couplet.

  • Clairvoyant – You can sense the magic around you. You see auras of magic and can feel magic radiating off of items and spells. You get +1 to knowledge rolls for anything you can see or touch.

  • Legacy – One or both of your parents is a Wizard Academy graduate and a generous donor. You start with one extra item from the common loot table.

  • Scrappy – When all else fails, you aren't afraid to resort to good, old fashioned fisticuffs. You can make a physical attack against an enemy as an action, dealing 1 damage to them. The enemy must be within striking damage or you must have something potentially dangerous to throw.

  • Sturdy – You are remarkably resilient. You have +2 max HP.

  • Windborne – Whether with wings or an innate affinity for wind, you can fly.

Inventory

At the beginning of the game, each failed wizard has the following:

  • Hat – A standard-issue wide brimmed, pointy wizard's hat.

  • Robes – Loose fitting wizard's robes with long, wide sleeves.

  • Magical Conduit – An item a wizard uses to help focus their power, such as a staff, wand, crystal ball, amulet, or spell book.

  • One item from the common loot table.

Everything else a failed wizard may need on their adventurers must be acquired or conjured.

Playing the Game

Taking Turns

When the action gets intense enough that it's both unclear and important whose action goes first, then it's time to take turns. Before the first turn, everyone rolls a d6. If a character uses an action on their turn that requires a d6, they use the result of this first d6. As such, the lowest roll goes first — someone casting a shorter spell can blurt it out first. If a PC and an NPC roll the same result, the PC goes first — one benefit at being so bad at magic is that they can throw caution to the wind and act hastily. If two PCs roll the same result, they can decide among themselves which goes first. After the first turn, players keep the same turn order and don't have to roll dice in advance.

On your turn, you can move and take one action. An action can be casting a spell, using an item, or some other activity that you can perform within a few seconds. You can also make a knowledge roll once on each of your turns without using your action.

Casting Spells

As a failed wizard, you are only good enough to cast simple spells. In fact, you can only cast spells so simple that they can be described in only a few words.

To cast a spell, declare what wizard discipline you are drawing from (see below), then roll a d6 then add the penalty or bonus determined by your grade in that discipline on your final report card. You describe your spell using a number of words equal to the number you rolled or fewer. For instance, if you roll a 3, you could invoke a spell to "summon a key!" or "heal me!" but not "summon key to that door!" The spell then creates the effect you described, although anything omitted from your description is left to the Headmaster's discretion, at which point they should choose the most hilariously disastrous option possible. If a failed wizard simply invokes "summon key!" then it may simply summon the answer key to a test (which may be the very sort of thing that got them expelled from the Wizard Academy), or if a failed wizard invokes "fire tornado!" they or one of their allies may end up trapped inside of it.

If the result of your roll minus a penalty is 0 or less, you still attempt to cast a spell with a single word, but the HM can be even less generous than usual in their interpretation of the results, opting to misinterpret or even ignore the word you gave them.

If you attempt to cast a spell beyond your capabilities, it may produce a similar but lesser effect. For instance, invoking "Kill that dragon!" to try to kill a powerful, ancient dragon may simply harm it.

If the HM determines that the spell you wish to cast doesn't match the wizard discipline you chose to cast it, they can choose to either penalize you with a worse effect or to reinterpret the spell into a form that would fit the discipline you chose.

Shield
When all else fails, you can always cast "Shield!" This shield reliably prevents the next 1 damage you would take before the start of your next turn.

Wizard Disciplines

The eight wizard disciplines are: Astromancy, Conjuration, Elementalism, Enchantment, Illusion, Naturalism, Necromancy, and Transmogrification. Your skill in six of these disciplines are represented as the grades on your final report card. For each of these disciplines, you have a bonus or penalty based on that grade as listed in the table below.

You left the academy before you could take any classes from the other two disciplines, Astromancy and Transmogrification. Astromancy includes magic involving space and celestial bodies and is most often used for predicting the future or teleportation. Transmogrification is the magic of changing living beings into other forms, such as turning yourself into a giant spider. Your penalty to spells with these disciplines is always -2.

Grade Bonus
A +3
B +2
C +1
D +0
F -1

Reactions: Other Spellcasters and You

When someone else casts a spell, you can use your reaction to try to assist by channeling your magic into the spell or interfere with a counterspell. You have one reaction and regain it at the start of your turn.

Channel

When you channel your magic, roll a d6 and increase the number of words they can add to that spell by half the result (rounded down).

Counterspell

When you counterspell, roll a d6, then choose a number of words equal to half the result (rounded down) and add those words to the end of that spell to change its result.

Knowledge Rolls

Knowledge rolls work much like casting a spell — roll a d6 and adjust by the bonus or penalty of the relevant discipline — except instead of choosing words your HM gives you a number of words equal to the result either about a subject or in answer to a question.

Health and Harm

You have 5 Max HP (or 7 if you're Sturdy). Unless specified otherwise, each time something harms you, it reduces your HP by 1, and each time something heals you, it increases your HP by 1. Similarly, if you cast a spell to harm or heal, you deal 1 damage or heal 1 HP. When you reach 0 HP, you fall unconscious, and the next damage you take kills you.

Loot

Common Loot

d8 Common Item
1 Blank scroll
2 Class ring of protection
3 Diet Elixir
4 Pants of cargo
5 Scroll of extra credit
6 Wand of pointing

Blank scroll

A blank scroll suitable for recording a spell to be cast later, probably leftover from a homework assignment you never did. Writing a spell on a blank scroll takes 10 minutes. Roll twice for the spell and take the highest value, adjust with your bonus and penalty as usual, then record the desired words for the spell on the scroll. Casting the spell takes one action and destroys the scroll.

Class ring of protection

This ring prevents the first 1 damage you would take. It recharges after you go 1 turn without taking damage.

Diet Elixir

All of the healing, none of the calories. You can drink this drink as an action to recover 3 HP, or you can shotgun the drink, requiring no action but only restoring 1 HP.

Pants of cargo

These pants can store up to 1000 lbs. and 100 ft. by 100 ft. of stuff in a magical space within its many pockets, but the pants and all its contents always only weigh 1 lb. You can retrieve anything stored in a pocket by asking for it aloud then either grabbing it or dumping it out.

Scroll of extra credit

When you use this scroll, requiring no action, it increases your bonus for a discipline by 1 until after the next time you cast a spell, counterspell, or make a knowledge check for that discipline.

Wand of pointing

You can use this wand to point at the target of a spell in place of using the precious few words you have to cast the spell. It's also handy during slideshow presentations.

Uncommon Loot

d4 Uncommon Item
1 Arcane eraser
2 Cap of the tutor
3 Kit of first aid
4 Scroll: Hall of passage

Arcane eraser

This magic eraser has 1d4 charges. You can spend 1 charge to counterspell without using a reaction. Alternatively, you can spend 1 charge to destroy a scroll and, if it is being cast, prevent its effects. This second use does not work on scrolls written with the marker of permanence.

Cap of the tutor

You can use your reaction to give someone who can hear you your discipline bonus for a single spell, counterspell, or knowledge check.

Kit of first aid

This kit heals a wizard fully. Using it takes 10 minutes.

Scroll: Hall of passage

When used, this scroll creates a magic doorway to a room of your choice in the academy (except rooms where students are not allowed). Additionally, when presented to a hall monitor, this scroll prevents them from becoming hostile.
